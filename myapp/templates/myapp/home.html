Catholic Charismatic Renewal

The Charismatic Renewal in the Catholic Church for which Popes John XXIII and Paul VI and the fathers of the second Vatican council prayed and which Paul VI and John Paul II later had occasion to observe, and so address in such glowing terms, first started among Catholics in January 1967 at Duquesn University Pittsburgh, and at Notre Dame University, USA, and Since then quickly spread to all Catholic Dioceses all over the world. There are now numerous Catholic Charismatic Prayer Groups. By 1981 the statistics showed that it existed in over 101 countries. The renewal has held many International Conferences 1973, 1975, 1981, 1989 1993, 1998(in Rome) and 1978(in Dublin) and its leaders have been encouragingly received in audience by Popes Paul VI, John Paul II, Benedict XVI and Francis I.

The Catholic Charismatic Renewal(CCR) had a very modest beginning. The first experience occurred among small groups of University students in 1967 at Duquesne University, Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, University of Notre Dame, South Bend, Indiana both in the United State of America (USA). They had gathered to pray for a new Pentecost experience in their lives.
